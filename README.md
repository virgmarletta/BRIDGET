## Premise
BRIDGET is a novel Human-in-the-Loop Hybrid Decision Making framework capable of dynamically switching between two states based on diagnostic metrics accounting for human fatigue and systemic performance.
Whereas State-of-the-Art applications in Learning to Defer and Learning Together showcase high performance in a vacuum, a combination
of the two remains rather unexplored in the literature. To this end, BRIDGET aims to promote a complementary relationship between the two approaches, combining the “best of both worlds” in high risk decision making fields.

The core innovation of the framework is that the deferral triage policy is calibrated using a predictor and a rejector trained on the data generated by a co-evolutionary process.
By training the deferral system on co-labeled data emerging from the interaction between the human agent and the AI, BRIDGET continuously updates the knowledge base at each iteration. 

While this process effectively enhances the baseline predictor's performance, the overall systemic results are extremely dependent on the skepticism thresholds put in place during the co-evolutionary labeling process and the specific archetype of the human interacting within the system.
